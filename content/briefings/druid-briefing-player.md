## Player briefing: Druids in Qualihut (player-safe)

Druids in Qualihut treat magic like ecology: something you listen to, negotiate with, and pay for later. They care less about “power” and more about **cycle discipline**: when to grow, when to stop, when to burn, when to remember.

If you play (or travel with) a druid, you’ll see a few repeated habits:

- Watching the sky like sailors watch weather.
- Treating boundaries (groves, seasons, oaths) as real constraints, not vibes.
- Recording what matters in bodies and living memory, not in books.

### The Three Moons (the sky as a pressure system)

Most druids track nights the way merchants track prices: not for poetry, but because it changes what the world will tolerate.

- Canon overview: [The Three Moons](../environments/three-moons.md)

At the table, you can use this as roleplay texture:

- One moon makes rest come easily (or makes sleep dangerous).
- One moon makes blood and growth behave strangely (generous or brittle).
- One moon makes memory, endings, and “return” feel close (clean or contaminated).

### The Living Script (druid memory that can burn)

Some druid circles don’t trust paper to keep faith. They keep record in living bodies: tattoos as archive, rank, oath, grief, and proof.

- Player-safe details: [The Living Script](../magic/the-living-script.md)

### Magical tattoo ink (spellwork done through skin)

Not every tattoo is “Script.” Some tattoos are spellcraft: a working bound to flesh, harder to steal and harder to remove.

- Player-safe details: [Magical Tattoo Ink](../magic/spells/magical-tattoo-ink.md)

### Common rumors

- Elder druids can be “read” the way priests read scripture, if you know the practice.
- The best ink costs more than steel, and the worst ink leaves scars that still “itch” when magic is near.
- On certain moon-nights, tattoos look like they’re remembering something you aren’t.
