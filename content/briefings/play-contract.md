## Play contract (player-safe)

This campaign runs best when everyone shares expectations about tone, teamwork, and how secrets work. This is that agreement.

### Tone & themes

- Wonder and dread.
- Political absurdity.
- Scarcity and trade pressure.
- Secrets with consequences (not secrets as “gotchas”).

### What play looks like

- You begin as **competent professionals**, assembled into a new team under imperial bureaucracy and its cut-outs.
- Expect covert work, leverage, and paperwork: maps, ledgers, favors, and deniable violence.
- The world is a sandbox; choices move factions and change the map.
- Travel is dangerous by default; “routine long-distance travel” is a lie people tell to sell tickets.
- Magic is high-impact but not free; it has markets, custody chains, and second-order effects.
- Death is meaningful; treat resurrection as **rare/conditional at best**, not a build assumption.

### Party expectations

- Build a character who **wants to work with the group** (shared risk, shared cover).
- Chase leads and make calls with incomplete information; the world won’t wait for perfect certainty.
- Expect tradeoffs: you rarely get a clean win and a clean conscience.

### Secrets, conflict, and consent

- No player-to-player secrets: we keep everything open OOC; any secrecy is character-context only.
- Character secrets are welcome when they create **future decisions**, not table sabotage.
- No one uses secrets to remove another player’s agency without consent.
- Sabotage and PvP require explicit consent at the table (narrative conflict is fine; surprise mechanical betrayal is not).

### Safety & boundaries

The setting includes political cruelty, scarcity pressure, and moral compromise. We set clear boundaries in session zero and play within them.
