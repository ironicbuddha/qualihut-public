## Player briefing: how magic works (what your character can know)

In Qualihut, magic is real, useful, and never free.

Two things matter more than spell lists:

- Magic behaves like a **finite resource** you can refine, trade, and corrupt.
- Time is **not universal**: consensus time exists; Fey time does not.

### The everyday reality

- Most “magic” people experience is practical: wards on gates, blessings on contracts, rituals that keep a town’s water clean, and small protections that prevent a bad season from becoming a riot.
- Big magic exists, but it concentrates around institutions (guilds, churches, courts) and along dangerous routes (wilds, sea lanes, thresholds).

### Refined magic (money that can explode)

- Refined magic is power made portable—used like fuel, like currency, and like ordnance.
- It’s traded through seals, audits, and custody chains. If you touch it, you become interesting.

### Divine magic (miracles with bureaucracy)

- People pray because it helps. Priests also perform sanctioned rites that sometimes produce real effects.
- Those rites come with calendars, witnesses, and paperwork. The holy has logistics.

### Nature and liminal magic (roads, forests, thresholds)

- Druids and old practitioners treat magic like an ecology: listen, align, pay costs later.
- Thresholds and “thin places” can bend time, memory, and causality. Travel is dangerous because the world has teeth.

### What to assume as a player

- If a situation feels neat, someone is lying—or someone else already paid the cost.
- Magic solves problems and creates second-order problems immediately.
