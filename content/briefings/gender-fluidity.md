## Gender and orientation (player-safe)

In Qualihut, there is **no stigma** attached to gender fluidity, transness, or sexual orientation. People notice presentation the way they notice regional accents or guild rings: as information, not as a moral category.

This is partly cultural and partly institutional: the [Solar Church](../institutions/solar-church.md) is a legitimacy machine (calendars, witnesses, contracts, heir-records), not an afterlife-policing religion. There is **no heaven or hell** in the way some faiths argue about, so the Church has no doctrinal need to legislate private life as "sin" in exchange for promised salvation.

### Shamans (common and unremarkable)

Many spirit negotiators and rural ritual specialists (often called "shamans" in common speech) are gender fluid. In-world, this is treated as normal: a social flexibility that fits a job defined by mediation, thresholds, and speaking across categories.

### At the table

- Gender and orientation are not a "gotcha," a joke axis, or a stigma engine in this campaign.
- If you want a personal arc about identity, it is welcome, but it will be framed as *your character's life*, not as society's default cruelty.
